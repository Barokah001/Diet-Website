What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value calculated using a personâ€™s weight and height. It is a simple screening tool that helps categorize individuals into different weight statuses, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

Why Should You Know Your BMI?

Health Awareness: BMI offers a quick assessment of whether your weight is in a healthy range for your height.
Disease Risk: Knowing your BMI can indicate potential risks for weight-related conditions like diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension.
Lifestyle Adjustments: It serves as a starting point for setting realistic health and fitness goals.

How Can BMI Help Manage Overall Health?

Tracking Progress: By monitoring changes in BMI, you can gauge the effectiveness of lifestyle modifications such as improved diet or increased physical activity.
Guiding Recommendations: Health professionals often use BMI alongside other metrics to tailor dietary and fitness plans for individuals.
Preventative Health: Maintaining a healthy BMI reduces the likelihood of chronic illnesses and promotes overall well-being.
